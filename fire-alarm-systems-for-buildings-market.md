<p><p>Fire alarm systems for buildings are categorized into conventional and wireless systems. Conventional fire alarm systems use a wired network of detectors and control panels, providing reliable, cost-effective solutions for smaller buildings. In contrast, wireless fire alarm systems utilize radio frequency technology, allowing for easier installation and scalability, ideal for large or complex structures. Both types ensure timely fire detection and response, enhancing occupant safety while catering to varying building needs and regulations.</p></p>
