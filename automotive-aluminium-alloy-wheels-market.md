<p><p>The automotive aluminum alloy wheels market consists of three primary types: casting, forging, and other methods. Casting involves pouring molten aluminum into molds, creating lightweight and complex shapes, making it cost-effective for mass production. Forging, on the other hand, involves shaping heated aluminum under pressure, resulting in stronger and more durable wheels, often preferred for high-performance vehicles. "Other" methods may include processes like extrusion or machining, catering to niche applications or specialized designs that require unique properties and aesthetics.</p></p>
