<p><p>Food allergen testing kits are categorized into various types based on their detection methods. Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR)-based kits utilize genetic material analysis to identify allergens at a molecular level, offering high sensitivity and specificity. Immunoassay-based or ELISA kits rely on antigen-antibody reactions to detect specific proteins associated with allergens, providing reliable quantitative results. Other methods may include newer technologies like biosensors or lateral flow assays, diversifying the market and catering to different testing needs in food safety.</p></p>
