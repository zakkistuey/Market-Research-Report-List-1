<p><p>Idiopathic Intracranial Hypertension (IIH) treatment involves various medications. Acetazolamide is a carbonic anhydrase inhibitor that reduces cerebrospinal fluid production, lowering intracranial pressure. Methazolamide, similar in action, offers an alternative with differing side effects. Furosemide is a loop diuretic that helps decrease fluid retention. Topiramate, traditionally an anticonvulsant, may alleviate symptoms and reduce pressure. The “Others” category includes adjunctive therapies or alternative treatments that may be employed based on individual patient needs and responses.</p></p>
