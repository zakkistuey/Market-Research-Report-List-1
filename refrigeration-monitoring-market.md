<p><p>The refrigeration monitoring market encompasses two primary types: wired and wireless temperature monitoring systems. Wired temperature monitoring uses physical cables to connect sensors to a central system, offering stable and reliable data transmission. In contrast, wireless temperature monitoring employs radio frequency or Wi-Fi technology to transmit data, providing greater flexibility and easier installation. Both types aid in ensuring optimal refrigeration conditions, crucial for preserving perishable goods, and allow for remote monitoring and alerts to maintain compliance and efficiency.</p></p>
