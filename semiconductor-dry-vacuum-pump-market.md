<p><p>The semiconductor dry vacuum pump market comprises several types, including cam, claw, and screw pumps, along with other variants. Cam pumps use rotating mechanisms to create vacuum pressure, offering compact designs and high efficiency. Claw pumps utilize two claw-shaped rotors to compress gases, delivering low maintenance and reliable performance. Screw pumps operate by rotating helical screws to transport gases, providing high throughput and continuous operation. Other types may include diaphragm or scroll pumps, each designed for specific applications within the semiconductor manufacturing process.</p></p>
