<p><p>The analytical standards market is divided into organic and inorganic standards, catering to various industries like pharmaceuticals, environmental testing, and food safety. Organic standards primarily consist of carbon-containing compounds, often used to ensure the accuracy of analytical testing methods for organic substances. In contrast, inorganic standards encompass minerals, metals, and non-carbon-based substances, essential for assessing the purity and concentration of inorganic materials. Both types play a critical role in maintaining quality control and regulatory compliance in analytical laboratories.</p></p>
