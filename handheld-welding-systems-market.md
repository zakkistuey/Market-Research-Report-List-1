<p><p>Handheld welding systems can be categorized into two main types: ultrasonic and laser. Ultrasonic welding utilizes high-frequency sound waves to create friction and heat, enabling efficient bonding, particularly in plastics and thin materials. This method is known for its speed and precision. In contrast, laser welding employs focused laser beams to melt and join materials, offering deep penetrative welding and minimal heat-affected zones. Both types are favored for their portability, versatility, and ability to create strong, reliable welds in various applications.</p></p>
