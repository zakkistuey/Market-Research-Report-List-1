<p><p>The portable and wearable dialysis devices market comprises two primary types: peritoneal dialysis and hemodialysis. Peritoneal dialysis devices allow patients to perform the procedure at home by using the lining of the abdomen as a filter, offering flexibility and independence. Hemodialysis devices, on the other hand, typically require a clinic setting but are increasingly being developed for portability, enabling patients to receive treatment on-the-go. Both types aim to improve patient quality of life and treatment adherence.</p></p>
