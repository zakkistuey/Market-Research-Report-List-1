<p><p>Novel Oral Anticoagulants (NOACs) are medications used to prevent blood clots in conditions like atrial fibrillation and venous thromboembolism. They are categorized into two main types: Direct Thrombin Inhibitors, which block the action of thrombin to prevent clot formation, and Direct Factor Xa Inhibitors, which inhibit Factor Xa to disrupt the coagulation cascade. The NOAC market is growing due to their advantages over traditional anticoagulants, including predictable dosing, reduced monitoring needs, and lower risk of certain complications.</p></p>
