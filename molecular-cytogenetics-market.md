<p><ul><li>Non-Radioactive In Situ Hybridization</li><li>Comparative Genomic Hybridization</li><li>In Situ Hybridization</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The molecular cytogenetics market encompasses various techniques used to analyze chromosomes for genetic abnormalities. Non-radioactive in situ hybridization (ISH) employs fluorescent or enzymatic markers to visualize specific DNA sequences on chromosomes, ensuring safety and ease of use. Comparative genomic hybridization (CGH) allows for the detection of genomic imbalances by comparing test and reference DNA, providing insights into chromosomal alterations. Traditional in situ hybridization remains crucial for mapping gene locations, enhancing diagnostic capabilities in genetic research and clinical applications.</p></p>
